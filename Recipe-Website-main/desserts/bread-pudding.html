Bread Pudding

A classic dessert made with leftover bread, custard, and warm spices. It's soft, rich, and best served with a drizzle of vanilla sauce or whipped cream.

Prep: 15 mins
Cook: 45 mins
Serves: 6
Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 4 cups bread cubes (day-old or slightly stale)
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg (optional)
  • 2 tbsp butter (melted)
  • 1/4 cup raisins or chopped nuts (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1

    Prepare the Bread

    Preheat oven to 180¬∞C (350¬∞F). Grease a baking dish and fill it with bread cubes. Add raisins or nuts if using.

  2. 2

    Make the Custard

    In a bowl, whisk together milk, eggs, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and melted butter until well combined.

  3. 3

    Soak and Bake

    Pour the custard over the bread cubes. Press down gently so the bread absorbs the liquid. Let it rest for 10 minutes. Bake for 40‚Äì45 minutes until golden and set.

  4. 4

    Serve

    Let it cool slightly. Serve warm with vanilla sauce, ice cream, or whipped cream.

Chef's Tips

  • Use brioche or challah bread for extra richness.
  • You can add chocolate chips or caramel for extra flavor.
  • Let the pudding sit in the fridge overnight for a deeper flavor.
  • To reheat, warm in a microwave or cover and bake at 160¬∞C until heated through.
