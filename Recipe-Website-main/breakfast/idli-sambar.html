Idli with Sambar

Soft and fluffy steamed rice cakes served with a spicy and tangy lentil-based vegetable stew. A staple South Indian breakfast loved for its nutrition and simplicity.

Prep: 8 hrs (includes soaking & fermentation)
Cook: 45 mins
Serves: 4
Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for soaking & grinding
  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • Mixed vegetables (carrot, drumstick, etc.)
  • Tamarind pulp (2 tbsp)
  • Sambar powder (2 tbsp)
  • Mustard seeds, curry leaves, dry red chilies (for tempering)
  • Oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. 1

    Prepare the Idli Batter

    Soak rice and urad dal separately for 6 hours or overnight. Grind them into a smooth batter, mix, and allow to ferment for 8 hours or overnight.

  2. 2

    Steam the Idlis

    Grease idli molds, pour the batter into them, and steam for 10‚Äì12 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.

  3. 3

    Cook the Dal for Sambar

    Pressure cook toor dal with turmeric until soft. Mash it well and set aside.

  4. 4

    Prepare Sambar

    In a pot, saut√© onions and tomatoes. Add vegetables, sambar powder, tamarind pulp, and cooked dal. Simmer for 15‚Äì20 minutes.

  5. 5

    Tempering

    Heat oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, and dry red chilies. Pour this tempering into the sambar. Mix well and simmer for a few minutes.

Chef's Tips

  • Use freshly fermented batter for the softest idlis.
  • Adding a pinch of fenugreek seeds while soaking dal can help with fermentation.
  • Adjust sambar consistency with water as needed.
  • Serve hot with coconut chutney for a complete experience.
