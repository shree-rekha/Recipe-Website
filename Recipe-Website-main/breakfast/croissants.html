Buttery French Croissants

Flaky, golden, and buttery‚Äîclassic French croissants are the perfect treat for a Continental breakfast. Enjoy them fresh out of the oven with jam or chocolate spread.

Prep: 1 hr
Cook: 15 mins
Serves: 8
Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 1/4 cups warm milk
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter (cold)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. 1

    Make the Dough

    Mix flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. Add milk and knead until a smooth dough forms. Chill for 30 minutes.

  2. 2

    Laminate with Butter

    Roll dough out and place cold butter in the center. Fold and roll out three times to create flaky layers.

  3. 3

    Shape the Croissants

    Roll dough into a large rectangle and cut into triangles. Roll each triangle tightly to form a crescent shape.

  4. 4

    Proof

    Place croissants on a baking sheet and let them rise until puffy (about 1 hour).

  5. 5

    Bake

    Brush with egg wash and bake in a preheated oven at 375¬∞F (190¬∞C) for 15‚Äì18 minutes or until golden brown.

Chef's Tips

  • Use very cold butter for best lamination results.
  • Let the dough rest in the fridge between folds to avoid tearing.
  • Serve warm for maximum flakiness and flavor.
  • You can freeze unbaked shaped croissants for future use.
